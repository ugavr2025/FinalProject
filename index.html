Project Overview

This final project is the culmination of the skills you have developed throughout the semester. Working in teams, you will design, develop, and demonstrate a collaborative mixed or virtual reality application. The goal is to create a functional and polished experience that is designed to teach or practice a useful skill.

Your application should not be purely for entertainment, though it can and should be engaging. You will be evaluated on your project's functionality, technical quality, and your ability to effectively demonstrate your work in a public session.

Core Project Requirements

All projects must meet the following technical requirements to be considered for a passing grade. These elements form the foundation of your application.

  • Collaborative Application: The experience must be multi-user. Multiple people must be able to interact with each other or a shared virtual environment simultaneously.
  • AI-Driven Character: Your scene must contain at least one non-player character (NPC) driven by AI that users can interact with in a meaningful way (e.g., via voice, gestures, or actions).
  • UI System: There must be a user interface to start, configure, or reset the application. This should be intuitive and usable within VR/MR.
  • Avatars (for VR): If your application is fully virtual, each user must be represented by an avatar that reflects their position and movement.
  • Anchors (for MR): If your application is in mixed reality, you must use spatial anchors to ensure that virtual content remains correctly aligned and positioned in the real world across sessions and devices.

Grading Rubric

50%

Core Functionality

Emphasis on a minimally viable product. Does the application work as intended? Are all core requirements (collaboration, AI, UI) met and functional?

20%

Technical Quality & Polish

Emphasis on performance and stability. Does the application maintain a comfortable frame rate (FPS)? Is it free of major glitches or bugs?

20%

Final Presentation / Demo

Emphasis on functionality during the live demo session. Can you effectively showcase your project's features and purpose to an audience?

10%

Project Website

Emphasis on creating a good advertisement for your project. Is the website professional, up-to-date, and does it clearly communicate your project's value?
